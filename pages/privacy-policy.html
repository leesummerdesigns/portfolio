Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes Our policies and procedures regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when You use the BloodLog application (the "Service") and explains Your privacy rights and how the law protects You.

BloodLog is designed with a privacy-first approach. Health data entered into the Application is stored locally on Your device and is not transmitted to or stored on our servers.

By using the Service, You agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

This Privacy Policy is intended to comply with applicable data protection laws, including the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), and the California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA), where applicable.

Data Storage

BloodLog is designed to store user data locally on the user's device.

Health logs and measurements entered into the Application remain on Your device and are not transmitted to or stored on the Company's servers.

Because the data is stored locally, You are responsible for managing backups and protecting access to Your device. If the Application is removed from Your device, the locally stored data may also be removed.

Types of Data Collected

Health Data

The Application allows You to record and store health-related information such as blood measurements, notes, logs, and timestamps.

This information may be considered sensitive personal data under applicable privacy laws. BloodLog processes this information locally on Your device solely to provide the core functionality of the Application.

The Company does not collect, store, or analyze Your health data on its servers.

Health data is never sold or shared with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes.

Usage Data

BloodLog does not collect analytics related to Your health logs or personal entries.

However, limited technical information may be collected by platform providers (such as Apple App Store or Google Play Store) when You download, install, or purchase the Application. This information is processed according to the privacy policies of those platform providers.

Camera Access

If You choose to use features that require camera access, the Application may request permission to access Your device's camera or photo library.

This access is used solely for the functionality of the Application and the information remains stored on Your device.

You can enable or disable camera access at any time through Your device settings.

Use of Personal Data

The Company may use limited Personal Data for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain the Service — To enable core application functionality.
  • To manage Your account — To provide access to features within the Application.
  • To manage subscriptions — To verify subscription status and enable premium features.
  • To respond to support requests — To respond to questions or technical support inquiries submitted by users.

BloodLog does not sell, rent, or trade Personal Data.

Subscriptions and Payments

The Application may offer paid subscription plans, including monthly and yearly subscriptions.

Payments are processed by third-party platform providers such as Apple App Store or Google Play Store depending on the device used.

The Company does not store credit card numbers or payment information.

The Application may receive limited information from the platform provider, such as confirmation of payment and subscription status, in order to manage access to premium features.

Payment processing is governed by the privacy policies of the respective platform providers.

  • Consent — You provide consent for certain features such as camera access.

  • Legal obligations — Processing may be required to comply with applicable laws.
